3D Printing & Miniature Painting

One of my favorite pastimes is 3D printing and painting miniature figures. Whether it's characters for tabletop games or custom designs, I love the process of creating intricate, detailed models. From designing to printing, and finally painting, each miniature is a work of art that brings my gaming adventures to life.

My 3D printer has allowed me to build an extensive collection of mini figures that I use in various tabletop role-playing games. Painting these figures is a meditative process that adds a personal touch to each piece, making every gaming session that much more immersive.


Dungeon Mastering & Tabletop Role-Playing Games

Running Dungeons & Dragons (D&D) and other tabletop RPGs is another big part of my life. As a Dungeon Master, I craft stories, build worlds, and guide players through epic adventures. I enjoy creating immersive experiences with detailed campaigns and hand-crafted settings, especially at my venue, the Bone Sack Speakeasy, where we host regular game nights.

My passion for storytelling and creativity comes to life when I’m behind the screen. I love the collaboration between players, the unpredictability of dice rolls, and the feeling of bringing a world to life. It's not just about the game—it's about the shared experience and memories we create.


Industrial Rock Enthusiast

When I'm not 3D printing or running games, I'm blasting industrial rock. Bands like Nine Inch Nails, Ministry, and Rammstein fuel my creativity and inspire the gritty, dark worlds I often explore in my RPG campaigns. The blend of electronic beats, distorted guitars, and powerful lyrics gives me the energy and inspiration I need to stay motivated in both my creative and professional endeavors.

Music plays a huge role in setting the mood, not just in life but also in my D&D sessions. A well-curated industrial rock playlist can turn a regular game night into a dark, intense adventure.
